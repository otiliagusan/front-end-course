Biography

Agatha Christie, born on September 15, 1890, in Torquay, Devon, England, is renowned as the "Queen of Mystery." Her life and career are a testament to her unparalleled talent and enduring legacy in the world of mystery and detective fiction. Here are five key points that highlight her remarkable contributions and intriguing personal history.

  • Prolific Career:

    • Agatha Christie wrote 66 detective novels, 14 short story collections, and numerous plays, solidifying her status as the "Queen of Mystery."

  • Iconic Characters:

    • She created two of the most famous fictional detectives, Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple, who have become enduring figures in the mystery genre.

  • Record-Breaking Play:

    • Her play "The Mousetrap" holds the record for the longest initial run of any play in history, having been performed continuously in London's West End since 1952.

  • Disappearance Mystery:

    • In 1926, Christie disappeared for 11 days, sparking a nationwide search and intense media speculation. The reasons behind her disappearance remain a mystery.

  • Honors and Legacy:

    • Christie was appointed a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire (DBE) in 1971. Her works have been translated into numerous languages and adapted into films, television series, and radio dramas, ensuring her legacy endures.
